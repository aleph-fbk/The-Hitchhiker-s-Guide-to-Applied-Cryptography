# Contributing to The Hitchhiker's Guide to Applied Cryptography

Thank you for your interest in improving *The Hitchhiker's Guide to Applied Cryptography* guidebook.

Contributions of all forms — corrections, clarifications, additional references, wording improvements, or structural suggestions — are welcome.

You may contribute (or contact us) via:

- **GitHub:** open issues or submit PR in this repository;
- **Anonymous feedback form:** <https://forms.gle/2KzxAmv3M1VnwUAVA>;
- **Email:** `hgac@fbk.eu`.

Please provide your contact information IF you want your contribution to be acknowledged.
We will do our best to review and respond to feedback and proposals for new notes, though our ability to address contributions may be limited by available time.

---

## Ways You Can Contribute

### 1. Report Issues or Errors
Please let us know if you encounter:
- typos or formatting inconsistencies;
- missing or unclear explanations;
- broken cross-references (`§`);
- missing or incomplete attribution;
- outdated terminology or examples.

### 2. Suggest Improvements
We welcome suggestions such as:
- clearer formulations;
- alternative explanations;
- additional high-level context;
- improved diagrams or examples;
- better linking across notes.

### 3. Propose New Notes
If a topic is missing and fits the scope of *applied* cryptography, feel free to propose a new note. Please include:
- a short abstract,
- motivation for inclusion,
- expected level of detail, and
- references or related material.

---

## Style and Scope Guidelines

To maintain coherence across the guidebook, note that:

- notes should remain **concise**, **self-contained**, and **pragmatic**;
- deep theoretical proofs or extensive mathematical derivations should be avoided;
- internal algorithm details should be included only when strictly necessary;
- cross-references to related notes should use the `§` notation;
- external references must be credible and appropriately cited;
- new concepts should be introduced using **bold text**;
- complex formalisms may be simplified, but any simplifications must be clearly highlighted.

---

## Attribution

If you contribute content drawn from an existing source, please include proper bibliographic information.
If any attribution in the guidebook is incomplete or incorrect, please report it — this will be corrected promptly.

---

## Code of Conduct

Please keep communication respectful, constructive, and aligned with the collaborative academic spirit of the project.

---
